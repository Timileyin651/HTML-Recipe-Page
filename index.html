Crispy Fried Chicken

Crispy fried chicken is a delicious dish with a crunchy golden coating and juicy meat inside. It is perfect for lunch, dinner, or special occasions.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 35 minutes
  • Servings: 8 people
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Skin cut up chicken pieces if you prefer.
  2. Place flour in a large plastic bag (let the amount of chicken you are cooking dictate the amount of flour you use). Season flour with paprika (which helps to brown the chicken), salt, and black pepper.
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk, then transfer, a few at a time, to the bag with flour; seal the bag and shake until well coated. Repeat with remaining chicken.
  4. Place coated chicken on a baking sheet or tray; cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper. let sit until the flour is a paste-like consistency. This is crucial!
  5. Fill a large skillet (cast iron is best) about ⅓ to ½ full with vegetable oil. Heat until VERY hot.
  6. Carefully lower as many chicken pieces into the skillet as it can hold. Fry in HOT oil on both sides until brown.
  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover skillet; cook for 30 minutes (the chicken will be cooked through but not crispy). Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue frying until crispy.
  8. Drain fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you may have to fry in a few batches. Keep the finished chicken in a slightly warm oven while frying the rest.

Tips / Notes

  • Allowing the buttermilk-flour mixture to come to a paste-like consistency is key to a crispiness on the outside and juiciness on the inside.
  • Paprika adds smoky flavor and has a low smoke point, which helps with browning the chicken.
